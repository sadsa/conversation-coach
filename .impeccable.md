# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Primary user learning Rioplatense Spanish, plus a few friends. The product organises around a three-pillar methodology that the user is now taught explicitly:

- **Practise** (`/`) — pick up a voice call with an AI persona, hold a casual chat, or share a WhatsApp voice note for analysis.
- **Review** (`/review`) — the inbox of recorded conversations and their annotated transcripts.
- **Study** (`/write`, kept as the URL — the label is now "Study") — the queue of corrections worth writing down on paper.

Use is contextual: a Practise session is a 5-minute deliberate window (sometimes morning, sometimes on the bus). Review is a focused daytime reading session after a real conversation. Study is the residue — the hand-written-flashcard cue.

### Brand Personality
**Patient, Encouraging, Spacious — with one voice that knows how to ring.**

The default register is a patient tutor that supports focused reading without rushing or overwhelming. Review and Study lean fully into that calm. The Practise voice surface is the one place we allow real warmth and invitation — the emerald call-pulse, the ringing-handset wiggle, the dynamic personas — because picking up a call is meant to feel alive, not clinical. Calm everywhere else; alive on the call.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light mode is the default for the dominant use case (daytime review), but dark mode is a first-class context, not a fallback.** Evening Practise sessions, late-night Study, and the iOS PWA's full status-bar treatment all live in dark mode. Tokens are tuned twice — chroma drops as lightness approaches the extremes so colour never reads garish (the dark Call tile drops chroma vs its light sibling but keeps the same brand emerald hue at the recognisable lightness).

**Technical and utilitarian in execution — clarity over decoration**, with carefully chosen warmth: warm cream (hue 75) for light, warm navy (hue 265) for dark — never neutral grey. The two body hues sit on opposite sides of the colour wheel, which is what makes the light/dark transition feel like a continuation rather than an inversion.

**References:**
- Google Recorder — clean transcript presentation, minimal chrome
- Gmail mobile — immediately intuitive navigation and actions
- Jive World — effective transcription display alongside audio context

**Anti-references:**
- Duolingo — no gamification, streaks, or playful mascots
- Generic admin dashboards — no dense data tables, no utilitarian grey boxes
- Overly playful language apps — no celebration animations, no emoji-heavy feedback
- "AI assistant" stock aesthetics — no neon-on-black, no purple-to-cyan gradients, no glowing accents used as a substitute for design

### Design Principles

1. **Readable first.** Large, comfortable text sizes. Generous line height. High contrast. The user has noted difficulty with small text — prioritise legibility over density. Body wrapped to ~70ch. `font-feature-settings: 'ss01', 'cv11'` is on by default for a friendlier lowercase `a` and a slashed zero; surfaces that need digit alignment (timers, durations) opt into `tabular-nums`.

2. **Scannable corrections.** Highlighted annotations must be immediately distinguishable during a quick scan. The eye should catch corrections without hunting. Three annotation states (unreviewed, saved, written) each get their own tinted-bg / underline-colour / text-colour triplet so state is legible from a metre away.

3. **Spacious layout.** Breathing room between elements. Avoid cramped cards or dense information blocks. White space is a feature, not waste. Sections breathe at `space-y-8` (32px); items inside a section stay tight at `space-y-3` (12px). Varied rhythm — tight inside groups, generous between them.

4. **Obvious actions.** Favoriting, tracking, and navigation should be self-evident. No hidden gestures or ambiguous icons. Swipe gestures (delete, toggle-read) are additive accelerators on top of an always-visible primary tap target — never the only way to do a thing.

5. **Informative without lecturing.** Explanations should be available but not forced. Let the user pull detail when they want it, not push it constantly. AnnotationSheet docks on demand; the methodology eyebrow names the pillars in three words and stops there.

6. **Visual weight signals the path.** When several actions sit together, deliberately vary their weight rather than treating them as peers. The Practise home has three doors — the emerald Call door is loud, Chat is quiet, Share is quietest — so the eye knows where to go first without needing to read every blurb. The pipeline stepper uses a quiet rail and a desaturated "done" tint so the active stage is the loudest item on the page. The Methodology eyebrow renders the active pillar in accent and the other two in tertiary. Counter-signal: when a row's job is purely orientation (the eyebrow, the page title), nothing on it should fight for attention — that's why the Study count badge was retired.

7. **Stable URLs, evolving vocabulary.** The user-facing surface for saved corrections is now called **Study** but the route stays `/write` (and the DB stays `practice_items`). The user-facing surface for the inbox is **Review** but the underlying entity is still `sessions`. We rename what users see; we don't rename what bookmarks, deep links, and migrations depend on. New surfaces inherit this rule.

### Typography

Hanken Grotesk (body) paired with Source Serif 4 (display) — both chosen against type defaults. Hanken is a warm humanist sans with friendly OpenType swaps; Source Serif 4's optical-size axis lets greetings and page H1s read as titles rather than oversized body text. Display headings tighten tracking (`-0.015em`) and leading (`1.15`). The pairing is intentional — sans body, serif title — because the product is reading-heavy: titles want a bookish register, body wants neutral readability.

Do not reach for Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta, or any of the other reflex defaults. They've been considered and rejected.

### Motion vocabulary

Four shared motion vocabularies — pick one before inventing a fifth.

- **`oa-*` (onboarding teaching choreography)** — `oa-touch` → `oa-sheet` → `oa-backdrop` → `oa-pulse`. 4-second one-shot. `animation-fill-mode: both` so reduced-motion users (clamped to 0.01ms globally) snap straight to a complete teaching frame.
- **`processing-graphic`** — the consolidated "we're working on it" waveform. One visual across audio transcription and Practise post-call analysis.
- **`cc-call-*`** — the ringing handset and emerald expanding ring on the Practise active screen. Loops until the persona connects. Reduced-motion users see neither (the component passes undefined animation).
- **`stage-in` / `sheet-up` / `sheet-in-right`** — quiet entrance for pipeline stages and docked sheets. Pair with `motion-safe:` always.

Easing: `--ease-out-quart` and `--ease-out-expo` for natural deceleration. No bounce, no elastic — they read dated.

### Surface constraints

#### Practise home (`/`)
- Always renders the same three doors (Call / Chat / Share). No empty state — there's nothing to be empty of. New users get the same surface as returning users.
- Three doors land in declining visual weight (loud Call, quiet Chat, quietest Share). Don't equalise them — the weight IS the recommendation.
- The greeting is in the target language ("Buen día" / "Bonjour" / etc.). H1 wears `font-display`. The Methodology eyebrow sits directly below.
- Don't add data widgets to this surface. The page is intentionally light — the conversations dashboard moved to `/review` and the Study count moved to the bottom-nav badge.

#### Review home empty state (was: Home empty state)
- This is where the no-recordings-yet state lives now (formerly at `/`).
- **Don't duplicate the wizard.** The richer teaching (animated phone-frame illustrations, paced steps) lives at `/onboarding`. The empty state should send users there, not paraphrase it as a flat numbered list.
- **No "1, 2, 3, 4 numbered cards" pattern.** It reads as templated AI onboarding and historically drifts out of sync with the real UI (e.g. step copy describing buttons that have since been renamed). Reach for one decisive action — start the tutorial, or upload — instead of a list.
- **Always offer a primary action in-flow.** A first-time user shouldn't have to hunt for a floating FAB to know what to do next; the empty-state section itself owns the first move.

#### Methodology eyebrow (`<MethodologyEyebrow>`)
- Three words, three pillars, one accent. Active pillar is `aria-current="page"` and renders in accent; others are tertiary links. No counts, no badges — its job is orientation, not notification.
- Lives directly under the page H1 on `/`, `/review`, and `/write`. Surfaces that don't belong to the methodology (Settings, Sessions detail, Practice/[id]/status) don't render it.

### Decision log (why we chose what we chose)

- **Warm cream + warm navy over neutral grey** — neutrals feel sterile for a daily-use personal app; warm cream and warm navy carry humanity. Tinting both palettes toward different hues lets light and dark feel like distinct rooms rather than an inverted toggle.
- **Violet (hue 285) as accent primary** — calmer and more focused than blue (too "tech"), warmer than teal, more interior than red/orange (too urgent). Reserved for primary actions, active states, and the accent ring; never used decoratively.
- **Emerald (hue 150) reserved for voice/Call surfaces** — the call-pulse, the Practise home Call door, the call-mode ringing screen. Emerald = picking up. Violet = paying attention. Two accents with non-overlapping jobs, so the visual language stays unambiguous.
- **Amber (hue 70) reserved for warnings only** — pipeline warnings, late-session timer fill. Never decorative.
- **Phosphor as the default icon family** (Icon component) — regular + fill duality powers nav active-state swap; six-weight system means we rarely leave the family. One-off deviations require a real reason (brand marks, or a glyph Phosphor genuinely lacks — Material Symbols' `waveform` is the current exception).
- **OKLCH for every colour** — perceptually uniform, so steps look equal; trivial to drop chroma at extreme lightness to avoid garish results.
- **Docked sheet, not modal, for transcript review** — `<AnnotationSheet>` and `<WriteSheet>` are bottom-anchored on mobile, side panels on desktop, no backdrop. Modals interrupt; sheets accompany. Reach for `Modal` only when an action is genuinely modal (destructive confirmation).
