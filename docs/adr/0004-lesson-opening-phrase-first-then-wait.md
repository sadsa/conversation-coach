# Lesson opening: name the phrase first, wait for learner acknowledgement before explaining

The Study lesson coach was front-loading the full Phase 1 explanation (correction rationale + examples + comprehension check) as a single opening burst before the learner had spoken. This was overwhelming, especially for new learners. We considered keeping the coach-speaks-first pattern but just capping the length. Instead we split the opening into two beats: the coach names the phrase in a single sentence, then waits for the learner to acknowledge before beginning Phase 1. The explanation of why the phrase matters is Phase 1's job, not the opening. This mirrors how a real teacher opens a lesson and gives the learner a moment to orient before the teaching begins. The tradeoff is a slightly slower cold-start — a learner who says nothing will stall — but a learner who is silent is almost certainly confused, not ready, so waiting is the correct behaviour.

## Considered Options

- **Cap and continue** — keep the single-burst opening but hard-cap it to 2–3 sentences. Rejected: still front-loads explanation before the learner has signalled readiness.
- **Wait for acknowledgement (chosen)** — name + hook → silence → learner responds → Phase 1 begins.
