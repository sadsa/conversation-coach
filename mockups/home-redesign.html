Home redesign · concept set 01

Practise becomes the home.

The home page leads with the active step of the methodology: have a conversation. From there it offers three doors — pick up a call, casual chat, or share a real voice note. The recent-conversations inbox moves to its own tab. The methodology becomes:

Practise → Review → Study

Three concepts below explore where the study reminder (corrections to write down) should sit on the practise-led home — the only open question after the redesign.

Concept A

Three doors, study chip below.

The three options are the entire page. The study reminder lives as a quiet single-row chip just above the nav — present, but never competing with the primary action.

+ Cleanest hierarchy + Easy to ignore when 0 − Study reminder is easy to miss

Decisions baked into all three concepts

Practise is the home (/)

The old recordings dashboard moves to a new Review tab (the inbox of conversations with feedback). The home itself holds three doors only.

Three doors, never more

Pick up a call · Casual chat · Share a voice note. The third door folds the WhatsApp-share tutorial up from the quiet text row beneath today's Practice card, treating it as a peer way to start.

Nav reorders to match the methodology

Practise → Review → Study → Settings. The current "Conversations" tab is renamed to Review; "Write" becomes Study. Labels match the pillars exactly.

No conversations feed on the home

The dashboard's "Your conversations" list lives entirely in the Review tab. The home is a launching pad, not a feed — once the user starts a session it's the only thing on the page.

Greeting + tagline kept

The warm time-of-day greeting ("Buenos días") stays — it's part of the patient/encouraging brand. Subhead becomes the question: "How do you want to practise?" not a status line.

In-progress sessions don't live here

Currently the home shows in-flight uploads in a strip. With the new shape, an upload immediately routes to the per-session status screen, and Review carries a small "Processing" group for any background ones — so the home never has to surface them.
