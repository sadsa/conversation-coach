Landing methodology · 3 directions

Make the three-step methodology more evident on the Practise home.

The current eyebrow (PRACTISE → REVIEW → STUDY, 11px uppercase) is correct but quiet. These three directions move along a spectrum: always-on persistence (Concept A), conceptual truth (Concept B — the methodology is a cycle, not a line), and teach-once-then-quiet (Concept C).

Each phone frame keeps the shipped greeting, the H2 question, and the three doors — only the orientation strip between greeting and doors changes.

Concept A · persistent rail

Numbered step rail under the greeting.

Three circular nodes joined by a thin rail. Active node sits in an accent ring; others are quietly outlined. Adds ~32px of vertical space over the current eyebrow but communicates ordering and where-you-are at a glance. Cohesive across /, /review, /write.

Concept B · the loop

Show the cycle, not the line.

A small linear three-node strip with a curved arrow underneath that returns from Study back to Practise. Names the truth of the methodology — Study feeds the next Practise via internalised corrections. Same vertical footprint as the rail; more conceptually accurate. Italic caption underneath teaches the loop ("…and back to practise.") on first arrival.

Concept C · teach-once expansion

Loud on first visit, calm forever after.

First visit: the eyebrow expands into a 3-card teaching strip with icon, pillar name, and a one-line description. A small "got it" link dismisses (or it auto-collapses after the first session ends). All subsequent visits show the current shipped one-line eyebrow. Honours principle 5 — informative without lecturing.

Recommendation

Concept C as the teaching moment + Concept A's rail as the always-on residue.

First visit: render Concept C's 3-card teaching strip. After the user taps "Got it" (or after the first practice session completes), the strip collapses into Concept A's numbered rail — slightly more legible than today's eyebrow, but quiet enough not to compete with the doors. Concept B's loop is a strong idea but reads more as marketing diagram than as in-app chrome — better suited to /onboarding or a future /how-it-works page.
