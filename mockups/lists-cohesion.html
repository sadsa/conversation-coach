Untouched: The visible Done button stays (Study's per-row action is non-destructive and benefits from being explicit, per principle #4 "obvious actions"). The row chrome was already the target — Review just adopted it.
Why this direction: the brand references in .impeccable.md are Gmail and Google Recorder (inbox metaphor), but those interfaces use full-bleed dividers because their rows have no per-row action surfaces. Study needs a permanent Done button, which doesn't sit cleanly in a divided list. Cards are the smallest move that gives both surfaces one row primitive AND keeps Study's trailing tap target legible. Swipe gestures (delete, toggle-read) work the same as before; the rounded overflow-hidden card now clips the swipe-reveal layers to the rounded shape so the gesture reads as "this card slides".
